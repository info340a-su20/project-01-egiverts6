Mental Health & COVID-19

Mental health is an incredibly important topic that has become more accepted and talked about in recent years. However, it still remains a traditionally “taboo” topic in our modern society. In light of the COVID-19 outbreak, billions of people were forced to quarantine themselves for months. The effects of the virus fears as well as long term isolation greatly affected mental health of people all over the world like never before. According to the Washington Post, “A federal emergency hotline for people in emotional distress registered a more than 1,000 percent increase in April compared with the same time last year.”

Anxiety Disorder

A group of mental illnesses that cause constant and overwhelming anxiety and fear.

Depression

A disorder characterized by persistently depressed mood or loss of interest in activities, causing significant impairment in daily life.

Eating Disorder

A group of mental illnesses related to persistent eating behaviors that negatively impact health, emotions and ability to function in in daily life.

Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder

An Illness characterized by unreasonable thoughts and fears (obsessions) that lead to compulsive behaviors.

Post Traumatic Stress Disorder

A disorder in which a person has difficulty recovering after experiencing or witnessing a terrifying event.
